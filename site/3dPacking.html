3D Rotation-Aware Greedy Packing Algorithm

Integrated with LiDAR & Mask R-CNN | Smart Warehousing Systems

Project Overview

This research addresses the 3D bin packing problem (3DBPP) by introducing a rotation-aware greedy heuristic. By optimizing item orientation across three axes, the system achieves a higher packing density than traditional fixed-orientation models.

Performance Gain

+20.4% Efficiency

Increase in volumetric utilization compared to baseline greedy approaches.

Technical Pipeline

The algorithm operates on a dynamic environmental feedback loop:

1. Object Recognition

Utilizes Mask R-CNN to identify cargo from camera feeds, extracting 2D masks to estimate initial 3D bounding volumes.

2. Spatial Mapping

LiDAR point clouds are processed to generate a voxel-based map of the available container space, ensuring real-world accuracy.

3. Geometric Fitting

A Greedy Search evaluates placement candidates, using VTK for collision detection and surface alignment optimization.
