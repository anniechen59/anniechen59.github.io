Randomized Competitive Greedy Algorithm for Flow Decomposition

Algorithm Design | Graph Theory | Combinatorial Optimization | Python

The Challenge: MFD Complexity

The Minimum Flow Decomposition (MFD) problem is NP-hard. Traditional deterministic greedy methods often lead to structural fragmentation, where extracting a "widest path" prematurely destroys the global graph structure, resulting in a sub-optimal, larger set of paths and cycles.

Key Innovations

  • Competitive Bidding: Paths and cycles compete dynamically based on bottleneck capacities.
  • Stochastic Perception: Injecting multiplicative noise to escape local minima.
  • Monte Carlo Search: Retaining the best solution from multiple randomized sequences.

Methodology & Novel Contributions

Instead of a fixed sequence, this algorithm treats path and cycle extraction as a dynamic competition, sensitive to the evolving residual graph.

Noisy Widest-Path

Uses a stochastic Dijkstra relaxation. By adding random noise to edge capacities, the algorithm explores diverse paths rather than just the single strongest one.

Cycle-First Preference

Modified competitive rules to favor cycle extraction earlier, preserving the structural integrity of the remaining flow network.

Structural Anti-Fragmentation

Replaces naive DFS with a get_best_cycle heuristic to ensure high-capacity cycles are prioritized before path removal.

Refinement & Optimization

  • Canonical Merging: Duplicate or rotated cycles are canonicalized and merged to minimize the final decomposition size.
  • Validity Pruning: Ensures all extracted structures maintain flow correctness and bottleneck deductions.
  • Complexity Handling: Efficiently manages the trade-off between randomized search iterations and time budget.
